Studying Bitcoin and altcoin correlations

We are dissecting major crypto currencies, like Bitcoin, into fundamenttal trends and compare their dynamic with conventional stocks. This post is intended to be a "live" document, that we will be updated regularly with new information and questions.

I have downloaded the tables with minute, hourly and daily information on BTC and ETH from https://www.CryptoDataDownload.com

Let's first load the Bitcoin tables and understand them

open/close gives us prices at the beginning/end of a given time period. High/low are the highest and lowest prices attained during a given time period, respectively. Unix is the unix timestamp or also known as "Epoch Time". We can use this to convert to your local timezone. Tradecount is simply the number of trades.

The tables have most recent dates on top, so let's reverse them for convenience. Following Machine Learning etiquette, we should think about normalizing data. As for prices, we can introduce another column which records relative price changes. It's called a return in trading.

It is interesting to see how trading volume is related to the price change (return). On a daily scale, correlation between absolute change and volume is quite low:

However, if we look at a hourly scale, correlation is bigger

On a minute scale, it is even more significant

This is probably due to high volatility of crypto assets. Prices can change a lot within several hours but correction usually follows on the same day, thus reducing the retturn while volume keeps accumulating.

We can study a particular example which occurred on 2018-12-05. Coinbase has moved almost 5% of all Bitcoins and 8% of all ETH (5 billion dollars in total) to a different cold wallet and it caused a bit of panic on the market:

The price has recovered in just about 15 days- an impossible level of correction for conventional stocks
